A Failed Race

2011-07-29    1049words    3minutes    Echo    Life_in_10s

EN#

The college entrance exam is over. The initial frenzy has gradually subsided into calm, so calm that it feels a bit empty.

The bookshelf is full of unopened books by Tsangyang Gyatso and Nalan Xingde, rewards I envisioned for myself during my second and third years of high school. But now, they are covered in dust. I’d rather gossip than glance at those literary books.

I opened “Ashes of Time,” and after five seconds, I clicked the red cross. Opened it again, and clicked again. The rough visuals deflated my once inflated expectations instantly. What a boring holiday!

I accidentally found a diary from after the class divisions in my third year of high school: “All for the college entrance exam.” “This is my first wish, and also the goal.” “Ten months left: end of summer, autumn, winter, spring, and then the exam.” “I want to end this summer perfectly.” “August 27, the first exam of the third year.” “Though I know it will be a bit rough, there’s no need to be discouraged.” “Every subject should get at least 80%. That way, I’ll get 600 points. What are you afraid of?” “You need to surpass yourself first, then others.” “I know you disdain many people, so you need to work harder.”

Even though it’s only been a year, the childish words from the past evoke some… feelings when read in today’s decadence. Is it… touching? Bitter? Or just sad?

More than ten years ago, that little girl with a runny nose could never have imagined having such arrogance when facing the college entrance exam. But after just two short days of tests, the paradise I imagined did not appear. I nervously checked answers, anxiously waited for scores, and ended up with a score as expressionless as mine.

In a daze, I enrolled in a school. I slept in until lunchtime every day. Occasionally, I passed by the high school in the evening. The sunset was crimson. Boys were sweating on the football field, girls sneaking out of school hurried back with dinners bought outside. Even the students doing cleaning were watched with envy by me. I leaned against those black iron bars and laughed for no reason.

How can I regain that scholarly spirit? If the third year of high school was a race, then my opening was grand, the process was calm, the climax was unforgettable, but the ending was a complete disaster.
